## Overview

Safe, reliable electrical systems are critical for every home. From simple outlet repairs to complete rewiring, our directory connects you with licensed electricians who ensure your home meets current electrical codes and safety standards.

## Common Electrical Services

- **Outlet and switch repair/installation** — Replacing faulty outlets, adding new outlets, installing GFCI outlets in kitchens and bathrooms.
- **Lighting installation** — Recessed lighting, ceiling fans, under-cabinet lighting, and outdoor lighting.
- **Panel upgrades** — Upgrading from older 100-amp panels to 200-amp panels to support modern electrical demands.
- **Whole-house rewiring** — Replacing outdated knob-and-tube or aluminum wiring with modern copper wiring.
- **EV charger installation** — Installing Level 2 electric vehicle charging stations in garages.
- **Generator installation** — Whole-house standby generators and manual transfer switches.
- **Smart home wiring** — Installing smart switches, thermostats, and structured wiring for home automation.
- **Safety inspections** — Comprehensive evaluation of your home's electrical system for hazards and code compliance.

## Electrical Safety Warning Signs

Never ignore these signs — each can indicate a serious hazard:

- Frequently tripping breakers
- Flickering or dimming lights
- Burning smell or discoloration around outlets
- Warm or hot outlet covers
- Buzzing sounds from outlets or the panel
- Shocks or tingling when touching switches
- Two-prong (ungrounded) outlets

## Choosing an Electrician

1. **Valid state electrical license** — Verify it is current and covers residential work.
2. **Insurance** — Liability and workers compensation coverage.
3. **Permits** — A reputable electrician will pull required permits and schedule inspections.
4. **References** — Check reviews and ask for recent residential project references.
5. **Written estimates** — Detailed quotes specifying scope, materials, and total cost.
